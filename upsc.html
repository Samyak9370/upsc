Weekly News Analysis

Topic: Reforming the Anti-Defection Law (10th Schedule)

1. Context

The Supreme Court recently commented on the 'powerlessness' of the judiciary when a Legislative Speaker delays decisions on anti-defection petitions. This has reignited the debate on the need for significant reforms in the Tenth Schedule of the Constitution.

2. Key Issues with the Current Law

  • Speaker's Partisan Role: The Speaker, often belonging to the ruling party, may delay disqualification proceedings for political reasons.
  • No Time Limit: The law does not specify a time frame for the Presiding Officer to decide on a defection case.
  • Wholesale vs. Retail Defection: The law penalizes individual defectors but allows 'wholesale' defections if two-thirds of a party's legislators merge with another party.

UPSC Concept Simplifier

Static Topic: Directive Principles of State Policy (DPSP)

DPSP are ideals which are meant to be kept in mind by the state when it formulates policies and enacts laws. They are non-justiciable in nature.

Socialist Principles

Promote social and economic justice. (e.g., Art 38, 39, 41, 42, 43)

Gandhian Principles

Reflect the programme of reconstruction enunciated by Gandhi. (e.g., Art 40, 43, 46, 47, 48)

Liberal-Intellectual

Represent the ideology of liberalism. (e.g., Art 44, 45, 48, 49, 50, 51)

üí° Key Distinction: FR vs DPSP

While Fundamental Rights (FRs) are legally enforceable (justiciable), DPSPs are not. FRs establish political democracy, while DPSPs aim for social and economic democracy.
